Stem-leaf plot

How to read a leaf-stem plot

definition:
  • The leaf contains the smallest digit or the ones digit in the number of points that each player scored; The stem contains the tens digit.
  • Usually the leaf contains the rightmost digit and the stem contains all of the other digit
  • Easy to distribution

Example: a statistician for a basketball team tracked the number of the points that each of the 12 players on the team had in one game. How many points did the team score?

stem leaf
0 0 0 2 4 7 7 9
1 1 3 8
2 0

players the tens of whose score should be:

the tens digit
0 00 00 02 04 07 07 09
1 11 11 13 18
2 20
  • Therefore: the total sum of the points: 20+18+13+11+11+9+7+7+4+2 = 40

Box-and-whisker plot

Median vs mean

definition:
  1. A median is the middle number when the numbers are ordered up.
    • where the number is odd, there is literally a middle number
    • if there are even numbers, then there are two middle numbers, an average has to be taken
  2. After the data is divided by the median into two parts:
    • the first quartile is the median number of the first part of the data (the lowver half of the data)
    • the third quartile is the median number of the second part of the data (the higher half of the data)

Classification of datas

Type of statistics discriptive Inferential

definitions

  • mean (arithmetic) vs median vs mode: different ways to measure the data
    • mean: add up all the numbers and divide by the numbers there are
    • median: sort the numbers and find the particular number in the middle
      • if there is no middle number
        • take the middle two numbers and take the arithmetic mean of the two numbers
    • mode: the most common number in the set - the frequency of a particular number to occurence in a number set

examples

a number set: 3,3,3,3,100

mean = 112/5
median = 3
mode = 3

a sample of male height from the population

mean = miu / m; the population mean
mean = x bar; the sample mean

µ = (the sum of each of the data point) / number of the population
x bar = (the sum of the sample elements) / number of the elements in the sample

Measurement of dispersion: Variance of a sample

Set A of numbers: 2,2,3,3
Set B of numbers: 0,0,5,5
What is the mean of either set? To what extent are the numbers dispersing from the mean in each set?

µ1 = 2.5; µ2 = 2.5

The distance of each of the data from the means is different.

variance is the way to measure the extent how the numbers are far off from the means

The variance ∂2 = sum of the square of difference between each element and µ

  • ∂12 = ((2-2.5)2 + (2-2.5)2 +(0-2.5)2+ (0-2.5)2 )/4 = 0.25
  • follow the same clue we may get ∂22 = 6.25
